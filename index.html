Hello World(6.30.2016)

Aright everyone, welcome to my blog.

What you see here is a real rough web layout that I just whipped up based of another website that I threw together for computer science class maybe a year or two ago. I have since then nearly forgotten basically everything about html and css. Needless to say, there was much copy-pasting from past files to here. Anyway, this crude design is fine, as all it needs to be is functional. I'll probably try to improve the aesthetics sometime in the future. I've already put off making this first blog post for a while now.

Speaking of the blog, I've made a goal to myself to get my life in order. Pretty much for the past year (or all of high-school even), I've pretty much slacked super hard and have just been going through my academics with just about absolute lack of motivation. I've always thought that working with software was the career path for me and working in school was just the robotic procedure for me to achieve that goal. Despite me taking the prerequisite courses and getting the "recommended" marks in them, I haven't really gone out of my way to learn more about software development and coding. I guess I never really felt the pressure from my lack of solid knowledge on software development and simply relied on the computer programming courses at my high school to completely teach me everything I need to know. Now I'm sitting in front of my computer screen, just a little more than a day after my graduation ceremony. I've had this nagging feeling that's been growing stronger as every day passes. I feel like despite me being offered-and accepting-admission into Software Engineering at the University of Waterloo, I completely lack the foundational knowledge and experience to succeed in the program.

And so the idea of this blog came to be. I would document the events prior to and during my time in university as a memoir to look back on, and maybe even serve as a resource to future aspiring students that intend to follow the same path. At the same time, I would continually develop this website as a way of learning more about front and back end website development.

And here it is. I wanted this post to be a introduction of sorts but it just kind of devolved into some sort of rambling that dragged on longer than I intended. Just wanted to get something done before I procrastinate myself past the point of no return.
