July Update & Road Trip! (7.23.2016)

It's been a little less than a month since I made a post but hardly anything has happened.

I've just been taking the little "intro to web development" course on the Codecademy website on the side just to get a refresher on the basics of front end stuff. But to be honest, I've been putting off to play more video games. Overwatch has been pretty much the only thing I've been doing in the past week since I quit my job. In hindsight, I had lots of time learn things as well even when I had a job as I only worked part time. Preeeetty sure that this lack of productivity is going to come back and bite me in the ass later.

Residence has been assigned by the uWaterloo about a week ago. Though I registered for residence with 3 other people together AND we all collectively selected the same residence priority choices, we still managed to get our last choice...CLV. Though I don't have anything against CLV, it's literally on the complete other side of campus. It's so far, it offers its own shuttle service to campus. Regardless, we don't really have any choice but to accept it, so tough luck. Software Engineering doesn't offer electives first year, so I don't really have to worry about that. Hack the North registration is just ending and I've got a couple of responses for teammates in the SE Facebook group. Hopefully we'll get in so I can get steamrolled already.

As for this website, pretty much nothing has changed. The things that I have relearned from Codecademy is hardly anything more than what I've already used for the layout that you see here. And, if it works it's fine right? I probably need to find a way to allow text input directly from the website for better convenience. Right now I've been using the really ghetto method of writing out the entire blog post right in the HTML editor, which has the added crude-ness of me not knowing how to enable text wrap and manually pressing ENTER every time I'm running out of screen space. Of course, that doesn't translate to a new on the actual website but the text wraps fine once it's on the page.

Anyways, this post has been getting pretty lengthy and I've been typing this up from the front seat of my family's car, as we travel down the 400. For the next 3 weeks, we'll be heading across the US to Yellowstone and the Grand Canyon. Once again, I'll put off prelearning all material that would help me at Waterloo. Feelsbadman.



Hello World (6.30.2016)

Aright everyone, welcome to my blog.

What you see here is a real rough web layout that I just whipped up based of another website that I threw together for computer science class maybe a year or two ago. I have since then nearly forgotten basically everything about html and css. Needless to say, there was much copy-pasting from past files to here. Anyway, this crude design is fine, as all it needs to be is functional. I'll probably try to improve the aesthetics sometime in the future. I've already put off making this first blog post for a while now.

Speaking of the blog, I've made a goal to myself to get my life in order. Pretty much for the past year (or all of high-school even), I've pretty much slacked super hard and have just been going through my academics with just about absolute lack of motivation. I've always thought that working with software was the career path for me and working in school was just the robotic procedure for me to achieve that goal. Despite me taking the prerequisite courses and getting the "recommended" marks in them, I haven't really gone out of my way to learn more about software development and coding. I guess I never really felt the pressure from my lack of solid knowledge on software development and simply relied on the computer programming courses at my high school to completely teach me everything I need to know. Now I'm sitting in front of my computer screen, just a little more than a day after my graduation ceremony. I've had this nagging feeling that's been growing stronger as every day passes. I feel like despite me being offered-and accepting-admission into Software Engineering at the University of Waterloo, I still lack the foundational knowledge and experience to succeed in the program.

And so the idea of this blog came to be. I would document the events prior to and during my time in university as a memoir to look back on, and maybe even serve as a resource to future aspiring students that intend to follow the same path. At the same time, I would continually develop this website as a way of learning more about front and back end website development.

And here it is. I wanted this post to be a introduction of sorts but it just kind of devolved into some sort of rambling that dragged on longer than I intended. Just wanted to get something done before I procrastinate myself past the point of no return.
